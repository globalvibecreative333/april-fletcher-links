Video recording outline: How to use ChatGPT to write your cleaning business description

Video topic

How to use ChatGPT to write what your cleaning business does

(so customers actually get it — and so you can reuse it everywhere)

Section 1

The setup — what we're doing and why (60–90 sec)

  • Open ChatGPT. Nothing else on screen yet.
  • Say out loud: "If you run a cleaning business, you've probably written something like 'We clean homes and offices. Call us today.' — and it's fine, but it's not doing any work for you."
  • Tell them what they'll walk away with: a short version, a medium version, and a long version they can use on their flyer, Google profile, Facebook page, wherever.
  • Tell them you'll also give them the exact prompts so they can do this again on their own.
Section 2

The example business — set the scene (30 sec)

  • You're going to use a made-up cleaning business as your example. Keep it simple — something like "Sparkle Clean STL."
  • Residential cleaning, St. Louis area, been around 3 years, owner-operated, 2 staff.
  • Type this into ChatGPT before your first prompt — just so the AI has something to work with.
Prompt 1

Get the short version — one or two sentences

I run a residential cleaning business in St. Louis called Sparkle Clean STL. We've been open 3 years, it's owner-operated with 2 staff, and we focus on homes. Write me a short 1–2 sentence business description I can use on a flyer or at the top of my Facebook page. Keep it plain and direct — no hype.

While it runs: talk through what you asked for and why "plain and direct" matters. Show the output. Read it out loud. Point out what's working.

Prompt 2

Get the medium version — a short paragraph

Now write a medium version — 3 to 4 sentences. This one should mention what kind of clients we serve, what makes us reliable, and end with something that makes someone want to reach out. Still no hype. Friendly but professional.

While it runs: explain that this length works for a Google Business profile, an "About" section on a simple website, or a printed rack card.

Prompt 3

Get the long version — a full "About us" blurb

Now write a longer version — about a paragraph, 6 to 8 sentences. This one is for the "About" page of a website or a detailed Facebook page description. Include who we serve, how long we've been doing this, what we take pride in, and a sentence that speaks directly to someone who's nervous about letting a stranger into their home.

While it runs: point out that "nervous about letting a stranger in" line — that's customer psychology, not just a description. That's what separates usable copy from generic copy.

Prompt 4

The refinement — personalize it

The owner's name is Dana. She started the business after leaving a corporate job because she wanted flexible hours to be home with her kids. Add one sentence to the medium version that reflects that — something real, not a Hallmark card.

This is the moment to explain: the more real detail you give ChatGPT, the more the output sounds like you — not like every other cleaning business in town.

Section 3

The close — what they just learned and what to do next (60 sec)

  • Recap what they now have: short, medium and long versions, plus a personalized one.
  • Remind them where to use each: flyer/Facebook header (short), Google Business/website About (medium), full website page or detailed directory listing (long).
  • Tell them the prompts work for any service business — not just cleaning. Salon, landscaping, tax prep, whatever they do.
  • Your CTA: "If you want me to do this for your specific business — your actual name, your actual services, your actual story — I offer a custom walkthrough for $300. Link is in the description."

Estimated total length

10–15 minutes. Long enough to be useful, short enough to hold attention on every platform.
