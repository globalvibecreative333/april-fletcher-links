Video 1

How to write a one-liner for your cleaning business with ChatGPT

For flyers, Facebook headers, door signs, anywhere short copy lives.

Open

Open ChatGPT. Say: "Most business owners have never written a one-liner — a single sentence that tells someone exactly what you do and why they should care. I'm going to show you how to get one in about two minutes."

The prompt

I run a residential cleaning business in St. Louis called Sparkle Clean STL. Owner-operated, 3 years open, we serve mostly families and working couples. Write me a one-sentence description I can put on a flyer, the top of my Facebook page or a door hanger. Keep it plain and direct — no hype, no exclamation points.

While it runs: explain why "no hype, no exclamation points" is in the prompt. That instruction tells ChatGPT the tone. If you leave it out, you get "We are PASSIONATE about clean!" Read the output, evaluate it out loud — does it sound like a real business or a template?

If you want to show a quick refinement

If the first output is close but not quite right, show one follow-up: "Make it a little warmer" or "Take out [specific phrase]." This shows them it's a conversation, not a one-shot deal.

Video 2

How to write a short paragraph description with ChatGPT

For website About sections, Facebook page descriptions, printed rack cards.

Open

Open ChatGPT. Say: "A one-liner tells people what you do. A short paragraph tells them why to pick you. I'm going to show you how to write one that actually sounds like your business."

The prompt

I run a residential cleaning business in St. Louis called Sparkle Clean STL. Owner-operated, 3 years open. The owner's name is Dana — she started it after leaving a corporate job so she could be home with her kids after school. We serve mostly families and working couples. Write a 3–4 sentence description for my Facebook page or website About section. It should say what we do, who we serve and end with something that makes someone want to reach out. Friendly but professional. No hype.

While it runs: point out that Dana's backstory is in this prompt. That's the detail that makes the output sound like a person, not a template. Show the output. Read it out loud. Point to the sentence that sounds most human.

Video 3

How to write a full About section with ChatGPT

For website About pages, detailed directory listings, anywhere you have room to tell the whole story.

Open

Open ChatGPT. Say: "Most cleaning business websites have an About page that says almost nothing. I'm going to show you how to write one that actually builds trust — especially with someone who's nervous about letting a stranger into their home."

The prompt

I run a residential cleaning business in St. Louis called Sparkle Clean STL. Owner-operated with 2 staff, 3 years open. The owner's name is Dana — she started it after leaving a corporate job so she could be home with her kids after school. We serve mostly families and working couples who don't have time to clean. Write a 6–8 sentence About section for my website. Include who we serve, how long we've been doing this, what we take pride in, and one sentence that speaks directly to someone who feels nervous about letting a stranger into their home. Warm, honest, no hype.

While it runs: talk about that "nervous about letting a stranger in" line. That's customer psychology — it names a real fear your customer has before they call you. Addressing it directly in your About section does more trust-building work than any list of services. Show the output. Read the whole thing out loud.

Close

Tell them this works for any service business — not just cleaning. Salon, landscaping, bookkeeping, tax prep, whatever. The same prompt structure applies. Then: "If yours is still sounding generic, watch my video on giving ChatGPT the real details about your business — that's the thing that changes everything. And if you want me to do this for your specific business, link is in the description."
